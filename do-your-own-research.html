What is the market talking about most right now? Capital rotates between narratives. Being in the right sector early is half the battle.
Check CoinGecko Categories, Kaito AI, X trending

When BTC dominance peaks and starts dropping, altcoin season begins. That's when small caps can 10x-100x rapidly.

Kaito AI, Santiment, CryptoPanic

Project Fundamentals
A project is only as strong as its team, product, and community. This phase separates real builders from hype merchants.
Team & Credibility
‚úì
Team is doxxed / publicly identifiable
Anonymous teams aren't automatically scams, but a doxxed team has more skin in the game and accountability. Check LinkedIn, past projects, GitHub handles.
‚úì
Founder has relevant track record
Have they shipped a successful product before? Been in the space long enough? Look for prior exits, contributions to known protocols, or real-world domain expertise.
‚úì
Reputable backers or VCs invested
Names like Multicoin, a16z, Binance Labs, or Coinbase Ventures signal that smart money has done the due diligence already. Not a guarantee, but a strong signal.
Product & Problem
‚úì
Solves a real, specific problem
Ask: what would break if this project disappeared? If the answer is "nothing," the token exists only to be traded ‚Äî not a great long-term sign.
‚úì
Product is live or has a credible testnet
Projects with working code are far less likely to vanish than whitepaper-only ideas. Check if there's a live app, testnet activity, or deployed contracts.
GitHub activity, project website, DeFiLlama TVL
‚úì
Blockchain is actually needed
Does this genuinely need to be on-chain, or could it work as a regular web app? Projects that need decentralization have stronger staying power.
‚úì
Active GitHub with recent commits
Real builders ship code consistently. A dead or sparse GitHub is a major warning. Look for commits in the last 30 days and multiple contributors.
GitHub repo, CryptoMiso developer activity tracker
Community & Ecosystem
‚úì
Community feels organic, not astroturfed
Join the Discord or Telegram. Real communities ask hard questions, debate, and build. Fake ones just post "wen moon" and nothing else.
‚úì
Real ecosystem integrations or partnerships
Are other protocols building on top of this? Do known companies or DAOs use it? Integrations are proof of external value recognition.

Tokenomics Deep Dive
Even the best project can be a terrible investment if the token is structured to dump on retail. Understand supply dynamics before any entry.
Supply Analysis
‚úì
Check fully diluted valuation vs. market cap
If circulating market cap is $50M but FDV is $2B, there's an enormous overhang of tokens yet to hit the market. A token at $0.10 with 90% supply locked could dump to $0.001 after unlock.
CoinGecko, CoinMarketCap ‚Äî compare FDV to mktcap
‚úì
Map all upcoming token unlock dates
Team, VC, and advisor unlocks create massive selling pressure. Know exactly when big unlocks happen so you're not holding when insiders exit.
TokenUnlocks.app, Vesting.xyz
‚úì
Inflation rate is sustainable
High emission staking rewards can dilute your position rapidly. Check annual inflation rate and whether token demand will outpace new supply.
Token Distribution
‚úì
No single wallet holds more than 10-15% of supply
Whale concentration = easy price manipulation. If 5 wallets hold 80% of the supply, those wallets are the market.
Etherscan token holders, BubbleMaps
‚úì
Team allocation is reasonable (under 20%)
Founders and team deserve equity, but if they hold 40%+ with short vesting, they have every incentive to dump and abandon the project.
‚úì
Token has real utility ‚Äî not just governance
Governance-only tokens rarely capture value. Look for fee sharing, staking rewards tied to actual revenue, or burn mechanisms that reduce supply over time.

On-Chain Analysis
The blockchain doesn't lie. On-chain data tells you what wallets are actually doing ‚Äî not what the marketing team says they're doing.
Growth Signals
‚úì
Growing unique wallet count (week over week)
New unique wallets interacting with the protocol means real user adoption, not just speculation. Consistent weekly growth is a strong early signal.
Dune Analytics, Nansen
‚úì
TVL (Total Value Locked) is growing
For DeFi projects, rising TVL = users trusting the protocol with real capital. Declining TVL despite price pumps is a red flag.
DeFiLlama
‚úì
Transaction volume is organic, not wash-traded
High volume can be artificially inflated by bots trading with themselves. Look for consistent daily active users, not just transaction count spikes.
Dune Analytics custom queries, Bubblemaps
‚úì
Smart money wallets are accumulating
When known profitable wallets (tracked by Nansen as "Smart Money") are buying a token, it's a very bullish on-chain signal. Watch for accumulation patterns.
Nansen Smart Money filter
‚úì
RSI is not already overbought (below 70)
If the token already pumped 500% in the last week, you might be buying the top rather than the early opportunity. Check RSI on daily chart.
TradingView
Security Checks
‚úì
Smart contract is verified and audited
Verified on Etherscan/BscScan means you can read the actual code. An audit by a reputable firm (Trail of Bits, CertiK, OpenZeppelin) is crucial ‚Äî not just any random audit.
Etherscan, BscScan, audit report links
‚úì
Liquidity is locked and sufficient
If liquidity isn't locked, the team can pull all liquidity (rug pull) at any time. Also check that there's enough liquidity that a moderate sell won't crash price 50%.
Team Finance, Unicrypt ‚Äî check lock duration
‚úì
Test sell transaction before entering
Honeypots let you buy but not sell. Use DEX tools to simulate a sell before committing real money ‚Äî this is non-negotiable for new tokens.
Honeypot.is, Token Sniffer

Red Flags ‚Äî Exit Immediately
These are deal-breakers. If you spot any of these, no amount of narrative or hype can save the investment. Walk away.
‚úï
Anonymous team making "100x guaranteed" promises
Real builders let the work speak for itself. If someone is promising exact returns, they're either lying or setting up an exit scam. No legitimate project guarantees price performance.
‚úï
Contract not verified on block explorer
Unverified contracts are a blackbox ‚Äî you can't see what the code actually does. The owner could have hidden admin functions that allow unlimited minting or ownership transfer.
‚úï
Honeypot detected ‚Äî cannot sell
The most brutal scam: you can buy, price goes up, you try to sell ‚Äî and the contract blocks it. Always simulate a sell on honeypot.is before buying any new token.
‚úï
Top 10 wallets hold 80%+ of supply
Extreme whale concentration means the price is fully controlled by a small group. One sell from the top holder can wipe out your position instantly.
‚úï
Price pumped with no product updates or news
Sudden price spikes with zero fundamental catalyst = coordinated pump before dump. Check if volume spike coincides with anything real, or if it's just social media noise.
‚úï
Influencers paid to shill with no disclosure
Coordinated influencer campaigns often dump within 48 hours of the initial pump. Multiple accounts posting the exact same phrases/memes = paid operation.
‚úï
No GitHub or dead/copied codebase
If there's no code to show, there's no product being built. Some projects fork another project's code and change the name ‚Äî that's not development, that's laziness at best and scam at worst.
‚úï
Large insider unlock coming within 90 days
If team or VC tokens unlock soon, they have strong incentive to hype price before selling. Check TokenUnlocks.app and avoid entering right before major unlock events.
